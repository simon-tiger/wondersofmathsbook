Some odd things about +

1 - 1 + 1 - 1

Let's take a look at the following sum:

1 - 1 + 1 - 1 + ...

If you think about it, there are actually 3 answers to this.

1. If you put the parentheses like this...

(1 - 1) + (1 - 1) + (1 - 1) + ... = 0

2. If you put the parentheses like this...

1 + (-1 + 1) + (-1 + 1) + (-1 + 1) + ... = 1

3, and weirdest of all. If you put no parentheses at all...

1 - 1 + 1 - 1 + ... = ½

And why? There are a couple of ways to prove that the answer is ½, but here's my favorite one...

If we stop at a 1, the answer is 1. If we stop at a -1, the answer is 0. But if we never stop, what's the answer? It's really simple, we just take the average of the two previous answers! Simple, it's ½.
